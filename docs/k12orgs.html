k12.org.original <- read_csv('/Users/kellyasche/Library/CloudStorage/GoogleDrive-kasche@ruralmn.org/My Drive/Research/FY25-27/FY26/OHE Journey to meaningful employment/Data/CRPD 2 (angelique.workman@state.mn.us)/K12Organization_cleaned.csv') %>%

k12.org.master <- k12.org.original %>%
  left_join(SiteCountyCode[,c(1,2)], by = c("SiteCountyCode" = "Code")) %>%
  drop_na(SiteCountyCode) %>%
  rename(county.name = `Code definition`) %>%
  select(K12OrganizationID, OrganizationName, county.name) %>%
  mutate(county.name = str_replace(county.name, " County", ""),
         county.name = str_replace(county.name, "Saint Louis", "St. Louis")) %>%
  filter(county.name != "Unknown") %>%
  select(K12OrganizationID, OrganizationName, county.name) %>%
  left_join(counties.regions.1, by = c("county.name" = "Name")) %>%
  select(-Dem_RUCA, -mif, -edr.simple)

enrollment.org.ne <- enrollment.original %>%
  left_join(k12.org.master, by = "K12OrganizationID") %>%
  filter(project.pr == "Southwest")

n.years <- original %>%
  select(PersonID, grad.year, K12OrganizationID) %>%
  rename(grad.org.id = K12OrganizationID) %>%
  left_join(enrollment.original[,c(1,2,3,4)], by = "PersonID") %>%
  left_join(k12.org.original[,c(1,2,3,7)], by = "K12OrganizationID") %>%
  mutate(year = str_sub(TimeID, 1, 4)) %>%
  distinct(PersonID, year, .keep_all = TRUE) %>%
  group_by(PersonID) %>%
  summarize(n.years.attended = n()) %>%
  ungroup()

master <- original %>%
  left_join(n.years, by = "PersonID")

Summary of K12Org

Lets summarize the graduates to see their high schools rural-ness and location. In addition, now that we have joined the graduates and high school location together, we can see if there are any statistically significant differences in the percentage of students based on the demographic information (female, free or reduced free lunch, etc…).

Summary of high school location

Lets breakdown the graduates by which RUCA category their high school was located.

Nearly 71% of the observations graduated from a county categorized as “town/rural mix”, followed by 15.1% that graduated from a high school in urban/town/rural mix counties and 14% from entirely rural counties.

Summary of student demographics

The following analysis looks to see if there are any statistical differences due to rural-ness and geographic location of the high school for the following graduate demographics;

  • Were eligible for free or reduced lunch at some point in the dataset
  • Are Female
  • Have been homeless at some point in the dataset
  • Had limited english proficiency at some point in the dataset
  • Had non english speakders in home at some point in the dataset
  • Participated in a PSEO course at some point in the dataset.

The cross-tabs below indicate that there are 4 demographic variables that have proportions statistically different across RUCA categories. Each of the variables are indicated with an asterisk.

  • Free or reduced lunch: the more rural a district is, the higher the percentage of graduates that qualified for free or reduced lunch.
  • Homeless: as a school district becomes more urban, the percentage of it’s graduates indicating they experienced homelessness at some point increases significantly.
  • Limited English: the entirely rural districts had a slightly higher percentage of graduates with limited English.
  • Non-English in home: town/rural mix districts had a significantly higher percentage of graduates with non-English speakers in the home.
  • PSEO: a significantly lower percentage of graduates from entirely rural districts participated in a PSEO program.
  • RUCA Free or Reduced Lunch
  • RUCA Female
  • RUCA Homeless
  • RUCA Limited English
  • RUCA non-english in home
  • RUCA PSEO

Although 36.8% of the total number of graduates in the dataset receive free or reduced lunch, the more rural school districts had a higher percentage - 45.1% for entirely rural and 40.1% for town/rural mix schools.
