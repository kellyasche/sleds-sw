The purpose of multiple correspondence analysis is to examine any patterns among the independent variables with the goal of eliminating variables that are redundant - essentially two variables that are explaining the same thing. This type of analysis is not meant to confirm any predictions, but rather explore the data and make decisions on what data should be included in the prediction model.


Independent variables

The master dataset is composed of 50,830 rows. Each row represents an individual that graduated (StatusEnd = 8 or 9) from a high school located in the Northeasy region (defined by project) between 2008 and 2023.

The master dataset is also composed of 114 columns. Columns mainly represent various socio-economic, demographic, and educational career characteristics of the individual as well as a few characteristics of the high school geographic from which they graduated (i.e. county unemployment). The variables for each individual is composed of a mix of categorical and numerical values. In this analysis, we will only be exploring categorical data.

Here is a list of all the categorical variables we will be examining in this analysis along with a description of each.

  • Demographics
    • grad.year: this is the year they graduated high school.

      • White = Caucasian and not Hispanic
    • LimitedEnglishProficiencyIndicator:
      • Y = identified as having limited English proficiency at some point between 10th and 12th grade, otherwise N.
    • HomelessIndicator:
      • Y = the individual was identified as homeless at any point between 10th and 12th grade, otherwise N.
    • economic.status:
      • 1 = the individual was eligible for free or reduced lunch (codes 1,2,4,5) at any point between 10th and 12th grade, otherwise 0.
    • SpecialEdStatus:
      • 1 = the individual required special education services at some point between 10th and 12th grade, otherwise 0.
    • non.english.home:
      • 1 = the individual was identified as having English as not the primary language spoken at home between 10th and 12th grad, otherwise 0.
    • english.learner:
      • 1 = the individual was identified as an “English learner” at least one time between 10th and 12th grade, otherwise 0.


  • High school characteristics
    • Dem_Desc: the RUCA category of the high school from which the individual graduated.


  • High school accomplishments
    • pseo.participant:

    • took.ACT: whether an individual took the ACT exam
      • Yes
      • No
    • ap.exam:
      • 1 = the individual took an AP exam at some point,
      • 0 = the individual did not take an AP exam.
    • cte.achievement: three indicators with
      • “CTE concentrator or completor” = self-explanatory,
      • “CTE participant” = they took a CTE course but was not a concentrator or completor, and,
      • “No CTE” = they didn’t take a CTE course
    • sat.taken:
      • 1 = individual took the SAT at some point between 10th and 12th grade, otherwise 0.


  • Post-secondary
    • attended.ps.within.first.year.hsgrad: the individual attended a post-secondary institution within the first year of graduating high school.
      • Yes = Attended PS within first year

      • NA = never attended PS or attended but only to withdraw.
    • attended.ps:
      • Yes = the individual attended a post-secondary education institution.
      • No = never attended post-secondary, or attended but only “W” or “D”.
    • ps.grad:
      • Yes = Graduated from a post-secondary institution by 2023
      • Attending ps = attending a post-secondary institution as of 2023
      • No = Not attending a post-secondary institution nor graduated from one as of 2023.

      • 2 - Private not-for-profit, 4-year or above

      • 4 - Public, 2-year
      • 5 - Private not-for-profit, 2-year
      • 6 - Private for-profit, 2-year
      • 7 - Public, less-than 2-year
      • 8 - Private not-for-profit, less-than 2-year
      • 9 - Private for-profit, less-than 2-year
      • 10 - multiple sectors (if attended multiple institutions)
      • 99 - Sector unknown (not active)
      • “Never attended ps” = the individual never attended a post-secondary institution
      • “Did not grad” = the individual attended a post-secondary institution but did not graduate.
    • highest.cred.level: an identifier of the highest credential earned by the PersonID. The categories are;
      • Did not graduate ps
      • Attending ps
      • Less than associate degree
      • Associate degree
      • Bachelors degree
      • Higher than bachelors degree.
    • ps.grad.location: this is the following;
      • ps.grad.region: graduated from a college in the same region as their high school, but not same EDR
      • ps.grad.MN: graduated from a college in MN but not same region or EDR
      • ps.grad.outside: graduated from a college outside MN, but not in region or EDR or Minnesota
      • Did not grad: attended but did not graduate college or attended but did not graduate
      • Attending ps: Currently attending post-secondary at grad.year.x


Frequencies

First, lets check to see there aren’t any variable categories with very low frequencies (less than 2%) which can distort the analysis.

The tables below shows there are a few to be concerned about.

MCA analysis

Eigenvalues - Demographic Variables

Overview The MCA on demographic variables produced 9 dimensions, collectively explaining 100% of the variance in the data.

Dimension Retention Using the average eigenvalue threshold (1/k = 0.167), Dimensions 1-5 are recommended for retention, collectively explaining 74.6% of the total variance. Dimension 1 is clearly the strongest, explaining 27.8% of variance, with a notable drop-off to subsequent dimensions which each explain approximately 11%.

Notes The relatively even spread of variance across Dimensions 2-5 suggests that demographic variation in this dataset is multidimensional, with no single dominant second axis of variation.

COS2 - Demographic Variables

Overview COS2 measures the quality of representation of each variable category across the five retained dimensions. Values closer to 1 indicate that a category is well represented in the retained dimensional space.

Well Represented Categories Race/ethnicity categories are the best represented across the five dimensions. Black (0.970), Asian/PI (0.930), Hispanic (0.827), and AI (0.829) all show very strong representation, indicating these categories are well captured by the retained dimensions. The language variables non.english.home (0.823) and White (0.750) are also well represented.

Moderately Represented Categories SpecialEdStatus (0.661), english.learner (0.647), and Gender (0.611) show moderate representation, suggesting some variation in these variables extends beyond the five retained dimensions.

Weakest Represented Categories economic.status is the weakest represented variable at 0.437, suggesting that economic variation among students is not well captured by the five retained dimensions and may operate somewhat independently of other demographic factors.

Notes The expected symmetry between binary category pairs (e.g., economic.status_0 and economic.status_1) is present throughout, confirming the technical integrity of the MCA results.

Contributions - Demographic Variables

Overview Contributions measure how much each variable category drives each retained dimension. Categories with higher contributions are the primary drivers of that dimension’s axis of variation.

Dimension 1 - English Language Learner / Hispanic Axis Dimension 1 is primarily driven by non.english.home_1 (27.2%), english.learner_1 (22.7%), and Hispanic (17.1%), indicating this axis captures variation among students who speak a non-English language at home, are English language learners, and are of Hispanic ethnicity. These three categories together account for over 67% of Dimension 1.

Dimension 2 - Special Education / Gender Axis Dimension 2 is dominated by SpecialEdStatus_1 (41.6%), with secondary contributions from Gender (F: 11.4%, M: 11.1%), suggesting this axis captures variation among students with special education status alongside an independent gender gradient.

Dimension 3 - American Indian / Gender Axis Dimension 3 is primarily driven by AI (48.9%) with notable secondary contributions from Gender (F: 14.3%, M: 14.0%), suggesting this axis captures variation unique to American Indian students alongside a gender gradient.

Dimension 4 - Multi-Minority Race/Ethnicity Axis Dimension 4 is shared across Black (33.8%), Hispanic (26.7%), and Asian/PI (19.1%) and AI (18.6%), suggesting this dimension captures a broader pattern of variation among minority race/ethnicity groups.

Dimension 5 - Black / Asian/PI Axis Dimension 5 is primarily driven by Black (46.6%) and Asian/PI (41.5%), suggesting this dimension captures residual variation that distinguishes these two groups from other minority race/ethnicity categories.

Notes The removal of unknown race/ethnicity individuals has produced a notably cleaner dimensional structure, particularly for Dimensions 3-5 which now tell a more interpretable story of variation among specific minority race/ethnicity groups.

Variables to keep and eliminate

Multiple Correspondence Analysis was conducted across four variable groups: demographic variables, high school characteristics, high school accomplishments, and postsecondary variables. The MCA did not identify any additional variables for elimination beyond the decisions already made during the initial frequency table review. The following changes were made prior to MCA:

Variables Dropped:

  • sat.taken — near-zero variance with only 0.5% of individuals having taken the SAT
  • HomelessIndicator — very sparse positive cases
  • LimitedEnglishProficiencyIndicator — lower counts compared to english.learner and non.english.home
  • grad.year.covid — redundant recoding of hs.grad.year
  • hs.grad.year — removed along with grad.year.covid to eliminate the temporal dimension from MCA
  • attended.ps.within.first.year.hsgrad — subsumed by other postsecondary variables

Variables Recoded:

  • ps.grad.InstitutionSector — sparse categories 3, 5, 6, 8, and 9 collapsed into a new category 11 (Private other)
  • RaceEthnicity — Unknown race/ethnicity individuals removed from the dataset prior to MCA

Data Decisions:

  • pseo.participant NA values — identified as representing the 2008-2010 graduation cohorts where PSEO data was unavailable due to data system limitations. These individuals are retained in the master dataset but excluded from any analysis involving pseo.participant.
